Privacy

Your privacy is important to us. It is Shakespeare Act by Act Limited's policy to respect your privacy if we need to collect any information from you across this website or from the Shakespeare Act by Act app.

We do not collect any personal information from you. If in the future we ever need to do so, we will only ask for it when we truly need it to provide a service to you. We will collect it by fair and lawful means, with your knowledge and consent. We will also let you know why we’re collecting it and how it will be used.

We do not retain or store your data.

We do not share any personally-identifying information publicly or with third-parties, except when required to by law.

This website uses Google Analytics to gage the effectiveness of its content. For information on how Google Analytics collects and processes data, please click on this link: https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites?hl=en-US.

For those who would rather not have tracking cookies placed on their devices, we hope you find the following information for some of the most popular browsers helpful:

Chrome:

With Chrome, you can send a “Do not track” request to websites. However, many websites ignore this.

1. On the top-right of your screen, click More (i.e. three dots on top of each other) > Settings.

2. Select Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data.

Turn on ‘Send a “Do not track” request with your browsing traffic’.

Please refer to the Google website for more detailed information.

Firefox:

The latest version of Firefox (as at 5 October 2020) includes Enhance Tracking Protection which is automatically turned on by default for all users. This blocks all known third-party tracking as part of Firefox’s ‘Standard’ setting.

For Firefox 65 (released 29 January 2019):

1. Click Menu > Content Blocking.

2. Choose from other Standard, Strict or Custom.

3. With Custom, you can choose to block trackers only in private windows or in all windows. You also have four options for which cookies to block.

Please refer to the Mozilla website for more detailed information for your particular browser.

Internet Explorer 11:

With Internet Explorer 11, you can send a “Do not track” request to websites. However, many websites ignore this.

1. In Internet Explorer, select the Tools button.

2. Click Safety > Turn on Do Not Track requests if it is not already turned on.

To enable Tracking Protection:

1. Go to Tools > Manage add-ons.

2. In Tracking Protection, click ‘Enable’.

Please refer to the Microsoft website for more detailed information.

Microsoft Edge:

The latest version of Microsoft Edge detects and blocks potentially harmful trackers.

1. Select Settings and more > Settings > Privacy and services.

2. Ensure that “Tracking prevention” is “On”.

3. Choose from one of the three levels of tracking prevention: Basic, Balanced or Strict.

Please refer to the Microsoft website for more detailed information.

Opera:

Since October 2019, Opera has enabled its users to block trackers. To block trackers in the latest version of Opera (as at 5 October 2020):

1. Go to Settings > Basic > Privacy protection.

2. Enable Block trackers.

Please refer to the Opera website for more detailed information.

Safari:

1. Select Safari > Prefences.

2. Select Privacy.

3. Select ‘Prevent cross-site tracking’.

Please refer to the Apple website for more information.
